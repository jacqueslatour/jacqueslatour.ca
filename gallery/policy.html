1. Purpose

This policy establishes the information security requirements for Clearwater Advisory Group Inc. ("the Corporation") to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets. It defines the framework for managing information security risks in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 and applicable regulatory requirements.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party service providers who access, process, store, or transmit information on behalf of the Corporation. It covers all information systems, networks, applications, and data repositories operated by or on behalf of the Corporation.

3. Roles and Responsibilities

3.1 Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)

The CISO is responsible for the development, implementation, and enforcement of this policy. The CISO shall report directly to the CEO on all matters related to information security.

3.2 IT Department

The IT Department shall implement technical controls as specified in this policy, manage cryptographic key material, maintain the Corporation's decentralized identifier (DID) infrastructure, and monitor systems for security events.

3.3 All Personnel

All personnel are responsible for complying with this policy, reporting security incidents promptly, and completing annual security awareness training.

4. Access Control

Access to information systems shall be granted on a need-to-know basis using the principle of least privilege. All access shall be uniquely identifiable and traceable to an individual.

  • Multi-factor authentication is required for all remote access
  • Privileged access shall be reviewed quarterly
  • Service accounts shall use key-based authentication where possible
  • Access shall be revoked within 24 hours of personnel departure

5. Cryptographic Controls

The Corporation shall employ cryptographic controls to protect the confidentiality and integrity of information in accordance with the following requirements:

5.1 Document Signing

  • All official documents shall be signed using Ed25519 digital signatures
  • Post-quantum signatures (ML-DSA-44) shall be applied alongside Ed25519
  • The Corporation's DID (did:web:clearwateradvisory.example) shall serve as the signing identity
  • Signed documents shall be accompanied by .vrfy verification manifests

5.2 Document Encryption

  • Confidential documents shall be encrypted using X25519-HKDF-AES256GCM
  • Encryption keys shall be derived from the recipient's published DID key agreement method
  • Encrypted files shall include .meta metadata for recipient key identification

5.3 Key Management

  • Cryptographic keys shall be generated using cryptographically secure random number generators
  • Private keys shall be stored in PEM format with file-system-level access controls (mode 600)
  • Key rotation shall occur annually or upon suspected compromise
  • DNSSEC trust anchors shall be maintained for the Corporation's domain

6. Incident Response

Information security incidents shall be reported immediately to the CISO via the designated incident reporting channel. The incident response procedure shall follow these phases:

  1. Detection: Identify and classify the incident
  2. Containment: Limit the impact and prevent further damage
  3. Investigation: Determine root cause and extent of impact
  4. Remediation: Eliminate the threat and restore systems
  5. Recovery: Return to normal operations with monitoring
  6. Lessons Learned: Document findings and update controls

7. Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed at least annually or when significant changes occur in the Corporation's operations, technology environment, or regulatory landscape. All revisions shall be approved by the CEO and communicated to all personnel within 30 days of adoption.
