Clearwater Advisory Group Inc.

Resolution of the Board of Directors

Authorization of Digital Trust Framework

Resolution No.: 2026-003 Date: February 12, 2026

At a duly convened meeting of the Board of Directors of Clearwater Advisory Group Inc. (the "Corporation"), held on February 12, 2026, at the registered office of the Corporation, a quorum being present and acting throughout, the following resolutions were adopted:

WHEREAS the Corporation provides compliance advisory and risk management services to regulated industries that require verifiable document integrity;
WHEREAS the Corporation has identified the need for a cryptographic document signing framework that provides independently verifiable proof of document authenticity, integrity, and provenance;
WHEREAS decentralized identifiers (DIDs) as specified by the W3C provide a standards-based mechanism for establishing verifiable digital identity without dependency on centralized certificate authorities;
WHEREAS the emergence of quantum computing necessitates the adoption of post-quantum cryptographic algorithms alongside classical signature schemes to ensure long-term document integrity;
WHEREAS the Board has reviewed and considered the technical assessment prepared by the Chief Technology Officer dated January 28, 2026;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT:

RESOLVED, that the Corporation hereby authorizes the adoption and implementation of a Digital Trust Framework based on the did:web decentralized identifier method, utilizing Ed25519 digital signatures for document signing and X25519 key agreement for document encryption;
RESOLVED FURTHER, that all official corporate documents, including but not limited to press releases, compliance certificates, policy documents, financial reports, and board resolutions, shall be digitally signed using the Corporation's registered DID and published with accompanying verification manifests;
RESOLVED FURTHER, that the Corporation shall maintain post-quantum cryptographic signatures (ML-DSA-44) on all signed documents in addition to classical Ed25519 signatures, ensuring document integrity against future quantum computing threats;
RESOLVED FURTHER, that the Chief Technology Officer is hereby authorized to establish DNSSEC trust anchors and register the Corporation with an appropriate Trust Registry to achieve Maximum Assurance status for the Corporation's digital identity;
RESOLVED FURTHER, that the officers of the Corporation are hereby authorized to execute any and all documents and take any and all actions as may be necessary or desirable to carry out the purposes and intent of the foregoing resolutions.
M. Thornton
Margaret Thornton
Corporate Secretary
J. Nakamura
James Nakamura
Chair of the Board
