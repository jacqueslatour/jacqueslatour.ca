Northwind Co-operative receives ISO 27001 certification

Ottawa, ON · April 18, 2026

Northwind Co-operative today announced that it has received ISO/IEC 27001:2022 certification for its information security management system, following a successful audit by an accredited third-party registrar. The certification covers all customer-facing services operated by the co-operative.

The scope of the certification spans the co-operative's Ottawa and Winnipeg data centres and includes its identity, membership, and document-management platforms. The initial certificate is valid for three years, with annual surveillance audits required to maintain good standing.

A deliberate, member-funded investment

"Our members asked us to prove — not assert — that their records are handled carefully," said Marie Tremblay, the co-operative's director of trust and safety. "Independent certification is the clearest way we have to do that. It also forces us to keep those commitments current, not just at the moment of audit."

The certification process took fourteen months and involved the review of approximately two hundred internal controls, including risk-assessment practices, incident response procedures, supplier management, and cryptographic key handling. No material non-conformities were identified during the final audit stage.

About Northwind Co-operative

Northwind Co-operative is a not-for-profit member organisation providing shared digital infrastructure to small community institutions across Canada. It is governed by an elected board of directors and holds no investor capital.
