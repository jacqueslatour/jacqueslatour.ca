Why it matters

Authenticity that doesn't rely on a gatekeeper

Every signature on this site anchors to a public decentralized identifier (did:web:jacqueslatour.ca) — which itself resolves over DNSSEC. No ledger, no blockchain, no account with a third-party platform. Just math, DNS, and a sidecar file.

  • The page is hashed, then signed

    A SHA-256 hash of the page content is signed with an Ed25519 private key (and an ML-DSA-44 post-quantum key). The result lands in a .vrfy sidecar next to the page.

  • The signer's public key is published at a DID

    The public half of the key is hosted at a public DID document at /.well-known/did.json, anchored to the site's DNS and TLS certificate.

  • Anyone can verify in seconds

    A verifier fetches the page and its sidecar, resolves the DID, checks the signature, and confirms the DNS anchor. The result is a cryptographic yes-or-no — not an assertion of trust.
